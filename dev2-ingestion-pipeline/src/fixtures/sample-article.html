Trump warns Iran over nuclear programme, says military option on table

By Jane Doe | April 4, 2026

President Donald Trump on Thursday warned Iran that the United States would use military force if Tehran did not reach a nuclear agreement, escalating pressure on the Islamic Republic ahead of scheduled talks.

"We are going to make a deal, or something very bad is going to happen," Trump told reporters at the White House. "Iran knows what I mean by that. They know very well."

The warning came as the United States and Iran were preparing for indirect negotiations in Oman, with European powers also urging Tehran to engage constructively with international demands over its nuclear programme.

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated that Tehran would not negotiate under pressure, saying "Iran will not accept any agreement that compromises our sovereignty or our right to peaceful nuclear energy."

According to Secretary of State Marco Rubio, "the window for diplomacy remains open, but it will not stay open indefinitely." He added that all options remained on the table.

White House National Security Adviser Mike Waltz confirmed that military planning for a potential strike on Iranian nuclear facilities had been updated, though he stressed that diplomacy remained the preferred path.

The Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly a fifth of global oil passes, has been at the centre of escalating tensions between Washington and Tehran over recent months.

Oil prices rose more than two percent on Thursday as markets reacted to the heightened rhetoric. Brent crude climbed above $90 per barrel for the first time this year.
